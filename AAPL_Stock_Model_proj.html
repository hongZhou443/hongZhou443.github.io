AAPL Stock Model

Photo by Nick Chong on Unsplash.com

Machine learning has been on a hot topic for several decades, with many industries employing people with relevant knowledge to explore and leverage the information and advantages machine learning can provide. Outside of Software, one particular industry where the effects of machine learning can be clearly imagined and, indeed seen, is that of finance. Looking at the rise of quantized trading firms centered around machine learning models and algorithms, I wanted to personally understand, at a high level, the ideas and programming behind these architectures. The AAPL_Stock_Model project is the end result. This article will go into detail how I built the project, the iterations of the code, as well as any interesting ideas I tried along the way.

Neural Networks and the Types of Models

The idea of machine learning has been in the back of my mind for several years, but it was really only halfway through freshman year of college that I really started looking into the concepts. However, through my peers and technical focused clubs, I had an initial direction to look into.

The first question I had going into the project was, how does machine learning actually work? The answer was math. A lot of it. However, behind the inscrutable numbers, one phrase kept popping up: neural networks. While the term might sound mythical, at its heart, a neural network is a type of function, albeit one that can get extremely complex. And, at the end of the day, a function is simply something that takes in inputs, and returns some outputs. From there, models are just different ways of using math to manipulate the inputs to get the ideal output. Simple right?

Now, lets get go into slightly more detail about the structure of neural networks and a conceptual overview (with minimal math, so don't worry) about how neural networks work.
